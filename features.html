DERBY CHAMPIONS AND THE BEST BREEDERS

Top 5 Filipino Gamefowl Breeders

Also known as RGA Gamefarm, Nene Abello Gamefarm is located at #1 Purok PH, Roxas Ave, Bacolod, Negros Occidental. The farm is the home of the famous Possum 226 SWEATER. The owner Nene Abello is probably one, if not the biggest name in the cockfighting industry. RGA Gamefarm has been winning national and international cockfights since 1992 and has won at least a dozen of derby championships. Rafael “Nene” Abello – one of the biggest names in gamefowl breeding in the country

Located at Balubad 2nd Rd, Silang, Cavite, Nene Aguilar Breeding Farm is another popular game farm founded by the late Las Piñas mayor Vergel “Nene” Aguilar. The farm is known not because the late Mayor owns it but because it has a handful of derby championship winnings.

AEJ Gamefarm is owned by Edwin Dela Cruz and is the winner of the 2017 World Slasher Cup. It is located at Brgy. Suplang, Tanauan City, Batangas The farm’s bloodline includes Golden Boy Sweater, 5K Sweater, Gilmore Hatch, Kearny Whitehackle, and Dom.

RED Gamefarm is a big gamefowl breeder located in Lucban, Quezon province. The farm claims to produce 1,200 battle-ready fighting cocks every year. It is owned by Raffy Campos and Edwin Arañez and can be contacted through their Facebook Page.

DL Gamefarm is owned by veterinarian Doyet Lapido in Catalunan Pequeño, Davao City. The farm produces gamefowl, offers K9 services, and has also an integrated farm.

Here are some general steps to breed roosters

Choose healthy, mature roosters with desirable traits such as good size, strong build, attractive plumage, and desirable behavior. Consider the breed standards and objectives you have in mind for the offspring.

Pair your selected roosters with compatible hens. Consider the genetic diversity, breed standards, and desired traits when selecting the hens.

Allow the roosters and hens to engage in natural mating. Ensure that the roosters and hens have sufficient privacy and space to carry out the mating process.

Ensure that the roosters and hens have appropriate housing and living conditions. Provide adequate space, proper nutrition, clean water, and a comfortable environment.

Monitor the breeding process and observe the behavior of the roosters and hens. Ensure that mating is occurring and that the roosters are successfully fertilizing the eggs.

Collect the fertilized eggs laid by the hens and place them in an incubator or under a broody hen for hatching. Follow proper incubation procedures to maximize the chances of successful hatching.

Monitor the incubation process, ensuring the temperature and humidity levels are appropriate. Follow the guidelines specific to the breed you are breeding.

Once the chicks hatch, provide them with suitable housing, proper nutrition, and care. Ensure they are protected from predators and provided with a clean and comfortable environment.
